What happens next

We'll process your application.

If you've provided a mobile number or email address, we'll keep you updated on your application's progress.

If you can get New Style JSA

We usually contact you within 14 days about an appointment to agree what you need to do to look for work.

This appointment will be with a work coach at your local Jobcentre Plus office and can last for up to 1 hour.

Check what proof of identity and other documentation you need to bring with you. If you need to bring someone with you to help, you can.

You must attend your appointment and agree your claimant commitment before we can pay you. We aim to pay within 7 days.

If you cannot get New Style JSA

We usually send you a letter within 14 days to explain why.

Contacting us

You only need to contact us to report a change of circumstances, such as starting a new job

Backdating your claim

In certain circumstances, you can start your claim from an earlier date than today. If your claim is eligible for backdating, you can still only get New Style JSA for up to 182 days (about 6 months) from your claim start date.

If you want to ask for backdating

There are 3 ways to get the JSA5 form, which you must complete and post back to us so that we can consider your claim for backdating.

To get the JSA5 form, you can either:

  • download the JSA5.pdf form (23Kb) and print at home or somewhere convenient
  • collect the JSA5 form from your local Jobcentre Plus office before or during your first appointment with a work coach
  • call the Benefit Enquiry Line and ask for the JSA5 form to be sent you by post Telephone: 0800 055 6688 Textphone: 0800 023 4888

Once you’ve completed the JSA5 form, you must send it to the following address:

Do you need help with housing costs or support for a partner or child?

If you're on a low income you might be able to apply for Universal Credit as part of a dual claim with New Style JSA.

Unlike New Style JSA, you can apply with a partner and get support for housing costs, children and childcare.

You or your partner must have less than £16,000 in savings or investments to be eligilbe.

Find out how to claim for Universal Credit on GOV.UK.
